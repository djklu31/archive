Lakehouse Entertainment


At the southend of Lake Washington, in the shadow of Mount Rainier and not far from a plane factory, sits a modest waterfront home built in 1939. Like most houses it has done what most houses do: providing comfort and shelter to working families. Then, in 2001, a special thing occurred. A young man moved into the home with his mom to take care of his elderly grandparents. It was every kid's dream. He wasted no time inviting friends over. They rowed boats and swam, and spent every day by the lakeside, soon discovering they were in love with kick flips and freestyles, as well, and so a collective of lake-based rappers and skaters began to take shape. BenadriLL and Yung DoLLa$ officially founded LakeHouse Ent, in 2009 with the release of a bedroom-recorded mixtape, "Trapper Slash Rapper." They rapped at skateparks, beaches, and abandoned banks, and wasted no time signing Mad Rich and Mo'Money. In 2013 the death of Ben's grandparents and a reverse mortgage forced LakeHouse Entertainment to sell their home. But with the help of the Lake God, and through the power of rapping, they will one day buy back the LakeHouse and return home.
